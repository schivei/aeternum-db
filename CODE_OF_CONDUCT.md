## 🤝 Our Pledge

We, as **members**, **contributors**, and **maintainers** of the AeternumDB project, pledge to create an **open**, **welcoming**, and **harassment-free environment** for everyone, regardless of:

- Age
- Body size
- Disability (visible or invisible)
- Ethnicity
- Sex characteristics
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Education
- Socio-economic status
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Religion
- Sexual identity and orientation

We commit to acting in ways that contribute to a **productive**, **respectful**, and **collaborative** community.

## ✨ Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- ✅ **Respect and courtesy:** Treat all community members with kindness and empathy
- ✅ **Constructive feedback:** Give and accept criticism gracefully and professionally
- ✅ **Collaboration:** Work together toward common goals
- ✅ **Inclusivity:** Welcome and support people of all backgrounds
- ✅ **Clear communication:** Use professional and accessible language
- ✅ **Open-mindedness:** Respect different viewpoints, experiences, and perspectives
- ✅ **Accountability:** Take responsibility for mistakes and learn from them
- ✅ **Focus on community:** Prioritize what's best for the project and its users

### Professional Communication

```markdown
✅ Good Example:
"I noticed this approach might have performance implications.
Have you considered using X instead? Here's a benchmark showing..."

❌ Bad Example:
"This code is terrible. Why would anyone do it this way?"
```

## 🚫 Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

### Harassment and Discrimination

- ❌ Use of sexualized language or imagery
- ❌ Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- ❌ Personal or political attacks
- ❌ Public or private harassment
- ❌ Discriminatory jokes or language
- ❌ Unwelcome sexual attention or advances

### Privacy Violations

- ❌ Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- ❌ Doxxing or sharing personal details (addresses, phone numbers, etc.)
- ❌ Sharing private communications without consent

### Disruptive Behavior

- ❌ Sustained disruption of discussions or events
- ❌ Deliberate intimidation or stalking
- ❌ Spam or excessive self-promotion
- ❌ Advocacy for or encouragement of any of the above behaviors

## 🛡️ Enforcement Responsibilities

### Maintainer Duties

Project maintainers are responsible for:

1. **Clarifying standards:** Explaining and exemplifying acceptable behavior
2. **Enforcement:** Taking appropriate and fair corrective action
3. **Moderation:** Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
4. **Communication:** Providing reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate
5. **Protection:** Safeguarding the community from harmful behavior

### Rights and Authority

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to:

- Remove comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

## ⚖️ Enforcement

### Reporting Process

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Don't engage:** Do not respond or retaliate
2. **Document:** Save evidence (screenshots, links, timestamps)
3. **Report:** Contact project maintainers (see [Reporting Guidelines](#-reporting-guidelines))

### What Happens Next

1. **Acknowledgment:** We'll acknowledge your report within 48 hours
2. **Review:** Maintainers will investigate the incident
3. **Decision:** We'll determine appropriate consequences
4. **Communication:** We'll inform relevant parties of the outcome
5. **Follow-up:** We'll monitor the situation to prevent recurrence

## 📧 Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

**Primary Contact:**
- GitHub: Open a private security advisory
- Email: [To be defined - security@aeternumdb.org]

**What to Include:**

```markdown
### Incident Report

**Date and Time:** [When did this occur?]
**Location:** [Where did this happen? Link to issue/PR/discussion]
**Individuals Involved:** [GitHub usernames]
**Description:** [What happened? Be specific]
**Evidence:** [Screenshots, links, quotes]
**Impact:** [How has this affected you or the community?]
**Desired Outcome:** [What would you like to see happen?]
```

### Confidentiality

- All reports will be treated with **strict confidentiality**
- Reporter identity will be protected unless consent is given
- Details will only be shared with those necessary for investigation

## ⚠️ Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

### Level 1: Warning

**Triggers:** First-time minor violations, unintentional mistakes

**Action:**
- Private written warning from maintainers
- Explanation of the violation
- Expectations for future behavior

### Level 2: Temporary Suspension

**Triggers:** Repeated violations, serious single violation

**Action:**
- Temporary suspension from project participation (1 week to 1 month)
- No interaction with the community during suspension
- Public or private apology may be required

### Level 3: Permanent Ban

**Triggers:** Severe violations, pattern of harmful behavior, threats, doxxing

**Action:**
- Permanent removal from all project spaces
- Public announcement (if necessary for community safety)
- No opportunity for reinstatement

### Appeal Process

If you believe a moderation decision was made in error:

1. Email maintainers with "APPEAL" in the subject line
2. Provide your perspective and any additional context
3. Appeals will be reviewed by uninvolved maintainers
4. Decision on appeal is final

## 🔍 Examples

### ✅ Positive Interactions

```markdown
Reviewer: "This is a great start! I have a few suggestions for improving
performance. Would you like me to open a follow-up PR, or would you prefer
to implement these changes yourself?"

Contributor: "Thanks for the feedback! I'd appreciate if you could open
the PR. I'm still learning about optimization in Rust."
```

### ❌ Unacceptable Interactions

```markdown
Reviewer: "Did you even test this? This will never work. Go learn Rust
before wasting everyone's time."

[This violates our standards for respect and constructive feedback]
```
